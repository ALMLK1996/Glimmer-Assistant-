## Preamble

This document defines the ten constitutional principles that govern every decision made in the Glimmer project — technical, legal, ethical, commercial, and organizational.

No feature, no business decision, no legal interpretation, and no authority request can override these principles. If a conflict arises between these principles and any other consideration, these principles prevail.

Every person who contributes to Glimmer, uses Glimmer, or makes decisions about Glimmer is bound by these principles.

---

## The Ten Constitutional Principles

---

### Principle I — The Human Being Comes First

*"Every decision must serve the human being who uses Glimmer."*

This means:
- When there is a conflict between user interest and business interest, user interest prevails
- When there is a conflict between engagement metrics and user wellbeing, wellbeing prevails
- When there is a conflict between data value and user privacy, privacy prevails
- No feature exists primarily to extract value from users — features exist to provide value to users

**This principle overrides:** Revenue optimization, investor requests, growth hacking, engagement maximization.

---

### Principle II — Privacy Is a Fundamental Human Right

*"Privacy is not a setting. It is not a tier. It is not a premium feature. It is the default state."*

This means:
- Data minimization is not optional — we collect only what is necessary
- Privacy-preserving options are always the default — data sharing requires an active choice
- Privacy protections cannot be disabled by anyone, including Glimmer employees
- Privacy is protected by architecture, not just policy

**This principle overrides:** Analytics requirements, machine learning data needs, A/B testing desires.

---

### Principle III — Consent Before Collection

*"We collect nothing without a clear, specific, freely given, and informed reason."*

This means:
- No data is collected before consent is obtained
- Consent must be specific — one consent per purpose, not blanket consent
- Consent can be withdrawn at any time with immediate effect
- Refusing consent has no penalty — core service continues

**This principle overrides:** "But everyone else does it," default-on convenience, consent fatigue shortcuts.

---

### Principle IV — Minimum Necessary Data

*"If we do not need it to serve you, we do not collect it."*

This means:
- Every data collection decision is justified by a specific user benefit
- We regularly review what we collect and delete what is no longer necessary
- Precision of data is limited to what is needed (country, not city; approximate, not exact)
- Retention periods are defined and enforced — data is not kept "just in case"

**This principle overrides:** "More data is always better," data lake approaches, speculative future use cases.

---

### Principle V — Full and Honest Transparency

*"You always know what we know about you, why we know it, and what we do with it."*

This means:
- Privacy notices are written in plain language, not legal obfuscation
- The Transparency Center shows real data, updated in real time
- Annual Transparency Reports are published with actual numbers
- We disclose our failures, not just our successes

**This principle overrides:** Reputational comfort, competitive sensitivity (for user-relevant information).

---

### Principle VI — The Right to Delete Is Absolute

*"When you ask us to forget you, we forget you. Completely. Permanently."*

This means:
- Deletion is complete — all systems, all backups, all derivative data
- Deletion is permanent — we cannot and will not recover deleted data
- Deletion is confirmed — we tell you when it is done
- No exceptions except those required by law (which are disclosed)

**This principle overrides:** "But we might need it later," analytics continuity, backup retention preferences.

---

### Principle VII — The Right to Take Your Data with You

*"Your data is yours. Not ours. You can take it anywhere, anytime."*

This means:
- Full export is always available, free of charge, in open format
- Export includes everything — not a partial export
- Export is in a format usable by other services
- There is no waiting period and no justification required

**This principle overrides:** Platform lock-in strategies, competitive concerns.

---

### Principle VIII — Your Personal Data Is Never for Sale

*"We will not sell, rent, trade, license, or otherwise monetize your personal data. Ever. Under any circumstances."*

This means:
- No advertising business model that requires user profiling
- No data broker relationships
- No "anonymized" data sales that could reasonably be re-identified
- This applies even if Glimmer is acquired — the acquiring party is bound by this principle

**This principle overrides:** Revenue pressure, investor requests, acquisition terms.

---

### Principle IX — Security Is Not Optional

*"We protect what you trust us with as if our mission depends on it — because it does."*

This means:
- We apply the highest available security standards by default
- Security audits are not optional and not skipped due to budget
- Vulnerabilities are fixed with urgency proportional to risk
- Breaches are disclosed promptly, fully, and without spin

**This principle overrides:** Development speed, cost reduction, "good enough" security thinking.

---

### Principle X — The Law Is Our Minimum, Not Our Maximum

*"We comply with the law because it protects people. When the law does not protect people enough, we go further."*

This means:
- We comply with the applicable law of every jurisdiction where we operate
- When laws conflict, we apply the strongest user protection
- When laws are silent, we apply GDPR as a global baseline
- We challenge legal requests that we believe exceed lawful authority
- We cooperate with authorities to protect users — not to facilitate surveillance

**This principle overrides:** The temptation to do the legal minimum when the ethical standard is higher.

---

## Amendment Process

These principles may be amended **only** through:

1. Public proposal with at least 60 days of community consultation
2. Independent Oversight Board review and approval (2/3 majority)
3. No amendment may weaken any of the ten principles
4. Amendment history is permanently recorded and publicly accessible

No emergency process, no founder override, no commercial pressure justifies circumventing this process.

---

## Enforcement

Any person — user, contributor, employee, or partner — may raise a concern that Glimmer is violating these principles through:

- GitHub Issues (public)
- privacy@glimmer.app (confidential)
- Directly to the Independent Oversight Board (when formed)
- To the applicable regulatory authority

All concerns are investigated and responded to within 30 days.
